Huns
The Huns were a nomadic people. They practiced Tengriism and used the Turkic script. Their spoken language was also Turkic. The Huns established their state from Lake Baikal to Tibet and from Eastern Turkestan to the Yellow River. Their army numbered 300-400 thousand. During the reign of Hulagu, in 47 BC, the Huns split into southern and northern branches. The southern Huns accepted Chinese rule, while the northern Huns allied with Central Asian tribes, migrated west, and retained their independence. Thousands of tourists have been introduced to Kazakh culture, cuisine, and history throughout the history of the Huns' existence. In my opinion, this is something we can truly be proud of!

About Us
EthnoVillage is a Kazakhstani tourist complex aimed at recreating ancient Hun settlements. Located near Almaty, it is a popular spot for tourists interested in learning about the history and culture of the ancient Huns who lived in this region. Visitors can explore the daily life of the Huns, their craft traditions, and enjoy the outdoor activities, beautiful views, and ancient atmosphere.
